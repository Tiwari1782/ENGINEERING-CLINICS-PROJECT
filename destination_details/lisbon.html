About Lisbon

Lisbon has quickly become one of the world's most popular destinations for digital nomads, and it's easy to see why. This charming Portuguese capital offers a perfect blend of old-world charm and modern innovation, all while maintaining an incredibly affordable cost of living compared to other Western European cities.

With over 300 days of sunshine per year, stunning coastal views, delicious food, and a thriving startup ecosystem, Lisbon provides everything a remote worker could want. The city's excellent infrastructure, fast internet, and abundance of coworking spaces make it an ideal base for digital nomads seeking both productivity and adventure.

Healthcare System

Portugal has a comprehensive public healthcare system (SNS - Serviço Nacional de Saúde). EU citizens can access it with EHIC. Private health insurance is recommended for non-EU citizens.

Safety Rating

Lisbon is one of Europe's safest capitals with a safety rating of 8.8/10. Petty theft can occur in touristy areas, but violent crime is rare.

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency (Police/Fire/Ambulance): 112
  • Tourist Police: +351 21 342 1623
  • Non-Emergency Police: +351 21 765 4242

Currency

The official currency is the Euro (€). ATMs are widely available throughout the city. Credit cards are accepted in most establishments.

Banking

Major banks: Millennium BCP, Novo Banco, Caixa Geral de Depósitos. Opening a Portuguese bank account requires a NIF (tax number) and proof of address.

Payment Methods

  • Credit/Debit cards widely accepted
  • Contactless payments common
  • MB Way - popular Portuguese mobile payment app
  • Cash still used in smaller establishments

Climate Overview

Lisbon enjoys a Mediterranean climate with mild, rainy winters and warm, dry summers. The city averages 300+ days of sunshine per year.

Average Temperatures

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 20-28°C (68-82°F)
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 8-15°C (46-59°F)
  • Spring/Fall: 15-22°C (59-72°F)

Best Time to Visit

March to May and September to October offer ideal weather with fewer tourists. Summer (June-August) is hot and crowded but perfect for beach lovers.

Best Neighborhoods for Nomads

Príncipe Real

Trendy and upscale neighborhood with beautiful gardens, boutique shops, and excellent restaurants. Popular with young professionals and creatives.

Cais do Sodré

Riverside neighborhood that's transformed from seedy to hip. Great nightlife, restaurants, and the famous Time Out Market. Close to coworking spaces.

Alfama

Lisbon's oldest neighborhood with narrow streets, traditional Fado music, and authentic Portuguese atmosphere. Charming but hilly.

Santos

Residential area with a local feel, antique shops, and riverside location. More affordable than Príncipe Real with good transport links.

Arroios

Multicultural neighborhood that's becoming increasingly popular. More affordable, authentic, with great restaurants and markets.

Getting Around

Lisbon's public transportation system is efficient and affordable, consisting of:

  • Metro: 4 lines covering main areas (operates 6:30am-1am)
  • Trams: Iconic yellow trams, including famous Tram 28
  • Buses: Extensive network throughout the city
  • Ferries: Cross the Tagus River to Cacilhas and other areas
  • Bikes/Scooters: Gira bikes and e-scooter rentals available

A monthly Navegante pass costs €40 and provides unlimited travel on all public transport in Lisbon. Uber and Bolt are also widely available and affordable.

Visa & Legal Requirements

EU/EEA Citizens: Can live and work freely in Portugal without restrictions.

Non-EU Citizens: Portugal offers several visa options for digital nomads:

  • D7 Visa (Passive Income): For those with stable passive income
  • D2 Visa (Entrepreneur): For starting a business in Portugal
  • Digital Nomad Visa: New visa specifically for remote workers (launched 2022)
  • Tourist Visa: 90 days visa-free for many nationalities

Portugal is known for having one of the more straightforward visa processes in Europe. Consider working with a relocation service or lawyer to navigate the process smoothly.

Top Coworking Spaces

Lisbon has a thriving coworking scene with options for every budget and style:

Second Home

Beautiful space in Cais do Sodré with a focus on design and community. Features rooftop terraces and event spaces.

Selina

Multiple locations combining coworking, accommodation, and social spaces. Perfect for nomads wanting an all-in-one solution.

CASA

Modern coworking space in Santos with flexible memberships, meeting rooms, and a friendly community atmosphere.

Heden

Boutique coworking space in Santa Apolónia with stunning river views, event spaces, and a rooftop bar.
