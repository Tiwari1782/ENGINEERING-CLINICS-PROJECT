About Prague

Prague is a stunning medieval city that looks like it jumped out of a fairy tale. With Gothic spires, baroque palaces, and the iconic Charles Bridge, it's one of Europe's most beautiful capitals. Beyond the postcard views, Prague offers excellent value, a growing tech scene, world-famous beer culture, and a compact, walkable center.

For digital nomads, Prague provides EU infrastructure at developing-world prices (though costs are rising). The city's central European location makes it perfect for exploring the continent, while its blend of history and modernity creates a unique atmosphere. Despite tourist crowds in the center and cold winters, Prague's charm, affordability, and quality of life make it an excellent nomad base.

Healthcare System

Czech Republic has good public healthcare. EU citizens can use EHIC for coverage. Private clinics offer English-speaking doctors and faster service. Healthcare quality is good and affordable. Travel insurance recommended for non-EU visitors.

Safety Rating

Prague is very safe (rating ~8.5/10). Violent crime is rare. Watch for pickpockets in tourist areas (Charles Bridge, Old Town Square, trams). Scams include overpriced taxis, strip club scams, and currency exchange tricks. Overall, one of Europe's safest cities.

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency (All Services): 112
  • Police: 158
  • Ambulance: 155
  • Fire: 150
  • Municipal Police: 156

Currency

The currency is Czech Crown (CZK, Kč). Despite being in EU, Czech Republic hasn't adopted Euro. ATMs widely available. Cards accepted in most places but cash preferred in smaller establishments and pubs.

Banking

Major banks: Česká spořitelna, ČSOB, Komerční banka, Raiffeisenbank. Opening a Czech bank account requires proof of residence. Many nomads use Wise, Revolut, or N26 for banking needs.

Payment Methods

  • Cards widely accepted in shops and restaurants
  • Cash preferred in traditional pubs and small shops
  • Contactless payments very common
  • Avoid currency exchange booths in tourist areas (terrible rates)
  • Use ATMs or bank exchanges for best rates

Climate Overview

Prague has a temperate oceanic climate with cold winters, warm summers, and distinct seasons. Snow is common in winter. Spring and fall offer pleasant temperatures. Summer can be hot with occasional thunderstorms.

Average Temperatures

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 15-25°C (59-77°F) - warm and pleasant
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): -5 to 2°C (23-36°F) - cold with snow
  • Spring/Fall: 5-18°C (41-64°F) - comfortable

Best Time to Visit

Late April-June and September-October offer the best weather with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer is busy but lovely. Winter is cold but magical with Christmas markets and snow.

Best Neighborhoods for Nomads

Vinohrady

Upscale residential area with parks, cafés, restaurants. Safe, quiet, and popular with expats. Good transport links and local feel.

Žižkov

Bohemian neighborhood with alternative vibe, cheap beer, and artistic community. More authentic and affordable than center. Close to Vinohrady.

Karlín

Revitalized industrial area with modern apartments, coworking spaces, and trendy cafés. Hipster central with good value for quality.

Holešovice

Creative district with galleries, markets (Manifesto), and industrial chic. More space, more affordable, growing nomad scene.

Nové Město (New Town)

Central but less touristy than Old Town. Good mix of residential and business. Close to everything with better prices.

Letná

Park area with beautiful views, relaxed atmosphere. Popular with families and young professionals. Great for work-life balance.

Getting Around

Prague has excellent, affordable public transport:

  • Metro: 3 lines (A, B, C) covering main areas, clean and efficient
  • Trams: Extensive network, most convenient for many areas
  • Buses: Serve outer areas and suburbs
  • Walking: Center is very walkable and beautiful
  • Bolt/Uber: Cheap for longer distances ($5-10 typical)
  • Rekola: Bike-sharing for short trips

Get a Lítačka card or use the PID Lítačka app for tickets. Monthly pass costs about 550 CZK ($25). Transport runs until midnight with night trams/buses after.

Visa & Legal Requirements

EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens: Can live and work freely in Czech Republic.

Non-EU Citizens:

  • Schengen Tourist Visa: 90 days in 180-day period (cannot work)
  • Long-term Visa: For work, study, or business (requires Czech employer/purpose)
  • Freelance Visa (Živnostenský list): For self-employed/freelancers (challenging to obtain)
  • Employee Card: Combined work and residence permit

Digital Nomad Reality: Czech Republic is strict about visa enforcement. Most non-EU nomads enter on tourist visas and do periodic "visa runs" to reset 90 days. For long-term, the freelance visa is possible but bureaucratic.
