About Medellín

Medellín has transformed from its troubled past into one of South America's most innovative and livable cities. Known as the "City of Eternal Spring" for its perfect year-round climate (72°F/22°C), Medellín offers digital nomads an unbeatable combination of affordability, modern infrastructure, friendly locals (paisas), and stunning mountain views.

The city's metro system, cable cars connecting hillside neighborhoods, and forward-thinking urban planning showcase its innovation. With world-class coworking spaces, a massive international community in El Poblado, delicious Colombian food, and welcoming culture, Medellín has become a top destination for remote workers. Safety has dramatically improved, though vigilance is still needed. The perfect weather alone makes it worth considering.

Healthcare System

Medellín has excellent private healthcare at affordable prices. Hospitals like Hospital Pablo Tobón Uribe and Clínica Las Américas offer high-quality care. Many doctors speak English. Travel insurance recommended, though cash payment is reasonable ($30-50 for doctor visits).

Safety Rating

Medellín's safety has improved dramatically (rating ~7.0/10) but use common sense. El Poblado is generally safe; avoid poorer neighborhoods. Don't flash valuables, use registered taxis/Uber, be cautious at night. Petty crime and scopolamine (drugging) scams exist. Tourist areas are well-policed.

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency (All Services): 123
  • Police: 112
  • Ambulance: 125
  • Fire: 119
  • Tourist Police: +57 (4) 444 5500

Currency

The currency is Colombian Peso (COP, $). ATMs available everywhere but charge fees. Cards accepted in most restaurants and shops in El Poblado, but cash is king elsewhere. Exchange rate fluctuates—monitor for best deals.

Banking

Major banks: Bancolombia, Banco de Bogotá, Davivienda. Opening a Colombian bank account requires cédula (Colombian ID) or extensive documentation. Most nomads use home banks + ATM withdrawals or services like Wise.

Payment Methods

  • Cash essential for street food, taxis, small shops
  • Cards accepted in malls, restaurants in El Poblado
  • ATMs charge 15,000-20,000 COP ($4-5) per withdrawal
  • Nequi/Daviplata - Colombian mobile payment apps (require local account)

Climate Overview

Medellín's nickname "City of Eternal Spring" is accurate — perfect weather year-round at 72°F (22°C) due to high altitude (5,000ft). Two rainy seasons bring afternoon showers but never extreme weather. Light jacket sometimes needed at night.

Average Temperatures

  • Year-round: 18-28°C (64-82°F) - consistently spring-like
  • Rainy Seasons: Apr-May and Oct-Nov (afternoon showers)
  • Dry Seasons: Dec-Mar and Jun-Sep (less rain)

Best Time to Visit

Any time is good! December-March is driest and most popular. Avoid September-November if you dislike rain. The consistent temperature means you can pack the same clothes year-round.

Best Neighborhoods for Nomads

El Poblado (Parque Lleras)

Digital nomad central with restaurants, bars, coworking, and nightlife. Safe, walkable, international. Most expensive but worth it for convenience and community.

Laureles

More authentic, residential neighborhood with local markets, parks, and growing expat presence. More affordable than Poblado with good restaurants and safer feel.

Envigado

Neighboring town (technically separate) with small-town feel, local culture, and lower prices. Good metro access to Medellín. Popular with budget nomads.

Manila (El Poblado)

Quieter part of El Poblado, more residential. Close to Parque Lleras but away from nightlife noise. Good balance of safety and tranquility.

Estadio

Up-and-coming area between Laureles and downtown. More local, affordable, with growing café scene. Good for those wanting authentic experience.

Belén

Residential area with universities, more budget-friendly. Less touristy but still safe. Good for those seeking local immersion.

Getting Around

Medellín has excellent public transport for a Latin American city:

  • Metro: Clean, efficient, safe. 2 lines connecting major areas
  • Metrocable: Cable cars connecting hillside neighborhoods with stunning views
  • Metroplus: Rapid bus system complementing metro
  • Uber/DiDi/InDriver: Cheap and essential ($2-5 most trips). Always use these over street taxis
  • EnCicla: Free public bike system (requires registration)
  • Walking: El Poblado and Laureles are walkable

Get a Cívica card for metro/Metrocable. Rides cost ~2,700 COP ($0.70). Uber is very affordable—most rides $2-5. Never take unlicensed taxis.

Visa & Legal Requirements

Tourist Visa: Most nationalities get 90 days visa-free (stamp on arrival). Extendable once for another 90 days at immigration office in Medellín (Edificio San Diego).

Visa Runs: Popular—fly to Panama, Ecuador, or Peru to reset your 90 days.

Digital Nomad Visa (2022): Colombia introduced a "V Digital Nomad" visa allowing up to 2 years. Requirements:

  • Proof of remote work contract
  • Minimum income: 3x Colombian minimum wage (~$750/month)
  • Application online through Cancillería website

Reality: Most nomads do tourist visa + extensions/visa runs. The digital nomad visa is worth it for long stays. Colombia is relatively lax about enforcement.

Top Coworking Spaces

Selina Medellín

Hostel + coworking in El Poblado with social atmosphere, rooftop, and strong nomad community. Great for networking and meeting other travelers.

Atom House

Beautiful coworking with multiple locations in Laureles and Envigado. Professional atmosphere, excellent amenities, and local community focus.

WeWork Medellín

Professional space in Oviedo Mall (El Poblado). Reliable, modern, good for meetings. More expensive but high quality.

Medellin Living

Hostel with coworking, social events, and tours. Party-friendly atmosphere. Good for younger nomads seeking social life.

Comunal Coworking

Local chain with locations in Laureles. Affordable monthly passes, good WiFi, meeting rooms. No-frills but reliable.

Cafés

Countless laptop-friendly cafés—Pergamino, Velvet, Café Zorba in Laureles. Colombians love coffee, so cafés welcome remote workers.
