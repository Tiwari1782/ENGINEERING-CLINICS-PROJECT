About Tokyo

Tokyo blends ultra-modern urbanism with deep tradition. It's one of the world's most connected cities — excellent public transit, ubiquitous high-speed internet, plentiful coworking spaces, and a huge international community make it attractive for digital nomads who value safety, convenience, and rich cultural experiences.

While living costs are higher than many Southeast Asian alternatives, Tokyo offers outstanding public services, cleanliness, and infrastructure. From quiet residential neighborhoods to neon-lit districts, the city provides diverse lifestyles and endless things to explore.

Healthcare System

Japan has excellent healthcare. Residents enroll in national or employer-based insurance; travelers should use travel insurance or short-term private policies. Medical facilities are modern, though English availability varies.

Safety Rating

Tokyo is one of the world's safest megacities (rating ~9.0/10). Violent crime is rare. Common-sense precautions (watch belongings in crowded areas) are usually sufficient.

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency (Police): 110
  • Ambulance / Fire: 119
  • English Helplines: Check municipal services for local support numbers

Currency

The official currency is the Japanese Yen (JPY, ¥). Cards are widely accepted in major stores and restaurants, but cash is still common for smaller vendors, izakayas and neighborhood shops.

Banking

Major banks include MUFG, SMBC, and Mizuho. International-friendly ATMs are available at 7-Eleven and post offices. Digital banking options are growing but often require residency documentation.

Payment Methods

  • Credit/debit cards accepted in major venues
  • IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) for transport and small purchases
  • Cash remains important for small merchants and markets

Climate Overview

Tokyo has four distinct seasons: hot, humid summers with a rainy (tsuyu) season in early summer, mild springs with cherry blossoms, pleasant autumns, and cool winters with infrequent snow.

Average Temperatures

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 22-31°C (72-88°F) - humid, rainy season in June/July
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 1-10°C (34-50°F) - cool and dry
  • Spring/Fall: 10-22°C (50-72°F) - most pleasant times

Best Time to Visit

Late March–May (cherry blossoms) and October–November (autumn foliage) are the best windows for weather and visuals.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • World-class public transport and punctuality
  • Extremely fast and reliable internet
  • Very safe and clean city
  • Rich food culture and convenience stores
  • Huge domestic and international travel connections
  • Abundant coworking and startup hubs

Cons

  • High living and rental costs in central wards
  • Language barrier outside tourist and business areas
  • Smaller apartment sizes compared to Western cities
  • Complex bureaucracy for long-term residency
  • Busy, crowded during peak commuting hours

Getting Around

Tokyo's transport network is one of the best in the world:

  • Subway & JR Lines: Dense network linking all major wards and suburbs
  • Buses: Useful for local routes not covered by trains
  • Taxis: Clean and safe but relatively expensive
  • Bikes & Walking: Many areas are walkable; bike-sharing is growing
  • IC Cards: Suica / Pasmo for seamless payments across transport and many shops

Use hyper-accurate transit apps for route planning and allow extra time during rush hours.

Visa & Legal Requirements

Japan does not offer a dedicated digital nomad visa. Visa options include:

  • Temporary Visitor: Short stays for many nationalities (often 90 days)
  • Working Visas (various E-series): Sponsorship by employer required
  • Highly Skilled Professional Visa: Points-based residence for high-skilled workers
  • Investor / Business Manager Visa: For entrepreneurs starting a business in Japan

If you plan long stays or working with Japanese clients, consult immigration lawyers or official immigration resources for exact requirements.
