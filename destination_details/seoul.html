About Seoul

Seoul is a dynamic metropolis where centuries-old palaces sit beside gleaming skyscrapers. It's one of Asia's tech and startup hubs, offering excellent infrastructure, some of the world's fastest consumer internet speeds, and a highly connected, English-friendly environment for international remote workers.

The city's efficient public transport, abundant coworking options, and diverse food and nightlife scenes make it a compelling base for digital nomads who enjoy fast-paced urban life. While Seoul can be pricier than many Southeast Asian cities, value for money is good compared to Tokyo or Singapore — and the cultural experiences, from traditional markets to K-culture, are exceptional.

Healthcare System

South Korea offers high-quality healthcare with modern hospitals and clinics. Residents on long-term visas enroll in the National Health Insurance (NHI). For short-term visitors or nomads, private travel insurance is recommended to avoid high out-of-pocket costs.

Safety Rating

Seoul is generally very safe (rating ~8.5/10). Violent crime rates are low; petty theft can occur in tourist areas. Nightlife districts are busy but well-policed. Be mindful of crowded subway lines during peak hours.

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency (Police/Fire/Ambulance): 112 (police) / 119 (fire & ambulance)
  • Tourist Emergency Helpline (English): +82-2-1330 (Korean government information hotline for some services)
  • Poison Control / Medical Info: check local hospital contacts

Currency

The official currency is the South Korean Won (KRW). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in the city; cash is useful for traditional markets and some small vendors. ATMs are common but check for international card fees.

Banking

Major banks include KEB Hana, Shinhan, KB Kookmin, and Woori. Digital banks like KakaoBank and K-Bank are popular for expats. Opening a local bank account typically requires an Alien Registration Card (ARC) or evidence of long-term stay.

Payment Methods

  • Contactless payments are widespread (NFC-enabled cards & mobile)
  • T-money card for public transport and small purchases
  • Many places accept international cards; keep some cash for markets

Climate Overview

Seoul has four distinct seasons: hot, humid summers (with a monsoon season in summer), crisp autumns, cold winters with snow, and mild springs. Seasonal festivals and changing scenery make each season unique.

Average Temperatures

  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 22-30°C (72-86°F), humid, monsoon rains in July
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): -6 to 5°C (21-41°F), cold and dry with occasional snow
  • Spring/Fall: 8-20°C (46-68°F) - comfortable and popular seasons

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) are the most pleasant times to visit — cherry blossoms in spring and colorful foliage in autumn.

Getting Around

Seoul's transport is efficient, clean and affordable:

  • Subway: Extensive, punctual, English signage — cheap and safe
  • Buses: Complement the subway network, route info available via apps
  • Taxis: Abundant and inexpensive compared to many Western cities — use Kakao T
  • Bikes & Scooters: Growing rental options for short trips
  • Intercity Trains: KTX for fast travel to Busan, Daegu, and beyond

Use a T-money card or mobile wallet for seamless payments across transport and convenience stores.

Visa & Legal Requirements

Short Visits: Many nationalities can enter Korea visa-free for short stays or use the K-ETA electronic authorization for eligible countries.

Longer Stays & Work: South Korea does not currently offer a dedicated digital nomad visa. Options include:

  • D-2: Student visa (for study)
  • D-8 / D-9: Investor / corporate visas for business activities
  • D-10: Job seeker visa
  • E-series: Work visas sponsored by employers (various categories)

For remote work, many nomads enter on short visas but consult immigration experts for legal compliance if you plan extended stays or to work with Korean clients.
